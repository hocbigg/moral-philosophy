# Summary

The Moral Philosophy (Ethics) curriculum is a **complete education in Moral Philosophy** using online materials.

Note: When there are courses or books that don't fit into the curriculum but are otherwise of high quality,
they belong in [extras/courses](extras/courses.md), [extras/readings](extras/readings.md) or [extras/other_curricula](extras/other_curricula.md).

**Process**. Students can work through the curriculum alone or in groups, in order or out of order.

- We recommend doing all courses in **Core**, only skipping a course when you are certain that you've already learned the material previously.
- For simplicity, we recommend working through courses (especially Core) in order from top to bottom. Some students choose to study multiple courses at a time in order to vary the material they are working on in a day/week.
- The courses in the Advanced section are electives. Choose one track to specialize in and complete all the courses listed under it.

# Curriculum

## Intro Moral Philosophy

| Subject | Why study? | Book | Online Course |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Introduction to Philosophy | Provides an overview of key philosophical questions, branches, and methods, setting the context for ethics by exploring concepts like truth, justice, and the good life. | [*Sophie's World* by Jostein Gaarder](https://archive.org/details/sophiesworldnove0000gaar_i9u8) (borrow on Archive.org) | [Introduction to Philosophy](https://www.coursera.org/learn/philosophy) |
| Critical Thinking and Logic | Develops skills in argumentation, fallacy detection, and logical analysis, essential for evaluating ethical claims and constructing coherent moral arguments. Prerequisite: None (can be studied concurrently with Introduction to Philosophy). | [*A Rulebook for Arguments* by Anthony Weston](https://archive.org/details/rulebookforargum0000west) (borrow on Archive.org) | [Logical and Critical Thinking](https://www.futurelearn.com/courses/logical-and-critical-thinking) |


## Core Moral Philosophy

| Subject | Why study? | Book | Online Course |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| History of Western Philosophy: Ancient and Medieval | Examines foundational thinkers (e.g., Plato, Aristotle, Aquinas) who shaped early ethical ideas, providing historical context for modern moral debates. Prerequisite: Introduction to Philosophy. | [*A History of Western Philosophy* by Bertrand Russell](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.125616) (borrow on Archive.org) | Covered in [Classics of Western Philosophy](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/24-01-classics-of-western-philosophy-spring-2016/) (MIT OpenCourseWare) |
| History of Western Philosophy: Modern and Contemporary | Explores Enlightenment and post-Enlightenment philosophers (e.g., Hume, Kant, Nietzsche) whose ideas directly influence contemporary ethics. Prerequisite: History of Western Philosophy: Ancient and Medieval. | [*A History of Western Philosophy* by Bertrand Russell](https://archive.org/details/in.ernet.dli.2015.125616) (borrow on Archive.org) | Covered in previous course; supplement with [Introduction to Western Philosophy](https://online.hillsdale.edu/courses/promo/introduction-to-western-philosophy) (Hillsdale College free online) |
| Epistemology | Investigates knowledge, belief, and justification, crucial for understanding how we know ethical truths and resolve moral disagreements. Prerequisite: Critical Thinking and Logic. | [*Epistemology: A Contemporary Introduction* by Alvin Goldman and Matthew McGrath](https://people.cs.rutgers.edu/~biglars/epis.pdf) (Amazon) | Covered in [Introduction to Philosophy](https://www.coursera.org/learn/philosophy) (University of Edinburgh on Coursera) |
| Metaphysics | Addresses questions of reality, existence, and human nature, which underpin ethical theories about free will, identity, and moral responsibility. Prerequisite: Introduction to Philosophy. | [*Metaphysics: An Introduction* by Alyssa Ney](https://www.amazon.com/Metaphysics-Introduction-Alyssa-Ney/dp/041564075X) (Amazon) | Covered in [Introduction to Philosophy](https://www.coursera.org/learn/philosophy) (University of Edinburgh on Coursera) |
| Metaethics | Analyzes the nature of ethical statements, moral realism vs. relativism, and the foundations of morality, bridging general philosophy to specific ethical theories. Prerequisite: Epistemology and History of Western Philosophy (both parts). | [*Metaethics: An Introduction* by Andrew Fisher](https://archive.org/details/metaethicsintrod0000fish) (Amazon) | Covered in [Introduction to Philosophy](https://www.coursera.org/learn/philosophy) (University of Edinburgh on Coursera) |
| Normative Ethics | Studies major frameworks for determining right action (e.g., utilitarianism, deontology, virtue ethics), forming the core toolkit for moral decision-making. Prerequisite: Metaethics. | [*Normative Ethics* by Shelly Kagan](https://www.amazon.com/Normative-Ethics-Dimensions-Philosophy-Kagan/dp/0813308461) (Amazon) | [Moralities of Everyday Life](https://www.coursera.org/learn/moralities) |

## Advanced Moral Philosophy

### Applied Ethics

Focuses on real-world moral issues in society and professions.

| Subject | Why study? | Book | Online Course |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Bioethics | Applies ethical theories to medical and biological dilemmas (e.g., euthanasia, genetic engineering), fostering practical moral reasoning in healthcare. | [*Bioethics: Principles, Issues, and Cases* by Lewis Vaughn](https://www.amazon.com/Bioethics-Principles-Issues-Cases-Vaughn/dp/0190903260) (Amazon) | [Introduction to Bioethics](https://www.edx.org/learn/ethics/georgetown-university-introduction-to-bioethics) (Georgetown on edX - free) |
| Environmental Ethics | Examines moral responsibilities toward nature, sustainability, and non-human entities, addressing global challenges like climate change. | [*Environmental Ethics: An Introduction* by Robin Attfield](https://www.amazon.com/Environmental-Ethics-Introduction-Robin-Attfield/dp/0745652530) (Amazon) | [Environmental Management & Ethics](https://www.coursera.org/learn/environmental-management-ethics)  |
| Business Ethics | Analyzes ethical decision-making in commerce, including corporate responsibility and fairness in economic systems. | [*Business Ethics: A Kantian Perspective* by Norman E. Bowie](https://www.amazon.com/Business-Ethics-Kantian-Perspective-Norman/dp/0631211748) (Amazon) | [Global Impact: Business Ethics](https://www.coursera.org/learn/business-ethics)  |

### Ethical Theory and Critique

Deepens theoretical analysis and critiques of moral systems.

| Subject | Why study? | Book | Online Course |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Feminist Ethics | Critiques traditional ethics through gender lenses, emphasizing care, relationships, and social justice. | [*Feminist Ethics* by Claudia Card (editor)](https://philpapers.org/rec/CARFE) (Amazon) | [Feminism and Social Justice](https://www.coursera.org/learn/feminism-social-justice) |
| Existentialist Ethics | Explores freedom, authenticity, and personal responsibility in thinkers like Sartre and Camus, challenging conventional moral norms. | [*Being and Nothingness* by Jean-Paul Sartre (focus on ethical sections)](https://www.amazon.com/Being-Nothingness-Jean-Paul-Sartre/dp/0671867806) (Amazon) | [An Introduction to the Principles of Existentialism](https://alison.com/course/an-introduction-to-the-principles-of-existentialism) |
| Relativism and Pluralism in Ethics | Investigates cultural and moral diversity, debating universal vs. relative ethics in a globalized world. | [*Moral Relativism: A Reader* by Paul K. Moser and Thomas L. Carson (editors)](https://www.amazon.com/Moral-Relativism-Reader-Paul-Moser/dp/0195131304) (Amazon) | [Relativism](https://www.coursera.org/learn/relativism) |

### Interdisciplinary Ethics

Integrates ethics with other fields like politics, science, and culture.

| Subject | Why study? | Book | Online Course |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Political Philosophy and Justice | Integrates ethics with governance, rights, and social contracts (e.g., Rawls, Nozick), essential for understanding moral foundations of society. | [*A Theory of Justice* by John Rawls](https://www.amazon.com/Theory-Justice-John-Rawls/dp/0674000781) (Amazon) | [Moral Foundations of Politics](https://www.coursera.org/learn/moral-politics) |
| Ethics of Technology and AI | Addresses moral implications of innovation, privacy, and artificial intelligence in modern life. | [*Ethics of Artificial Intelligence and Robotics*](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/ethics-ai/) (Amazon) | [Ethics of AI](https://ethics-of-ai.mooc.fi/) (University of Helsinki - free) |
| Comparative Ethics (Eastern and Western) | Compares moral traditions (e.g., Confucianism, Buddhism vs. Western ethics), promoting cross-cultural understanding. | [*Comparative Religious Ethics: A Narrative Approach* by Darrell J. Fasching et al.](https://archive.org/details/comparativerelig0000fasc) (Amazon) | [Eastern & Western Philosophy Documentary Series](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLy6vjBBz_zHRalbB3YEvO7gfG6SpVcMV-) |
